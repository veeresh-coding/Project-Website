Welcome to Smart Helmet

An innovative step towards rider safety — combining technology and awareness to protect lives on the road.

Real World Problem Explanation

Every year many people who ride two-wheelers get seriously hurt in crashes. Often the rider is knocked out or badly injured and cannot call for help. If the crash happens on a quiet road, in the night, or far from busy places, bystanders might not notice the injured rider for a long time. Even in cities, people sometimes do not know the exact location or they are unsure what to do, so calling an ambulance is delayed. A delay of just a few minutes can turn a treatable injury into a life-threatening condition. Delivery riders, students who travel alone, and people traveling at night are most at risk. Right now there is no simple, automatic system that detects a crash the moment it happens and immediately tells somebody where the rider is. Because of this gap, many preventable deaths and severe injuries happen every year.

Proposed Idea

The Smart Helmet is a low-cost safety device that automatically detects crashes and alerts emergency contacts with the rider’s location. It uses sensors like an accelerometer, gyroscope, and impact detector to identify accidents, along with GPS for tracking and Bluetooth or GSM for communication. When a crash is detected, the helmet beeps and vibrates for 30 seconds, giving the rider time to cancel if unharmed. If not canceled, it sends an SOS message with a Google Maps link and can flash an emergency light or call the first contact. The system minimizes false alerts by confirming signals from multiple sensors and includes affordable components costing around ₹1,500–₹4,000. This technology shortens response time and increases a rider’s chances of timely help and survival.

How It Works

1.While riding, sensors constantly watch for sudden changes (strong impact, fast fall, or helmet flipping).
2.If sensors detect a crash pattern, the helmet gives a loud beep and vibrates immediately.
3.A 30-second countdown begins and a small “Cancel” button lights up. If the rider presses it, the alarm stops (false alarm avoided).
4.If the rider cannot cancel, the helmet turns on the GPS, gets the location, and sends an SOS SMS with the location link to the saved emergency contacts.
